About

               Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam, known as the Missile Man of India, was a visionary leader, brilliant scientist, and the 11th President of India. His life was a true reflection of dedication, humility, and passion for the nation's progress. Known for his work in India's missile programs and his advocacy for education, he inspired millions of young minds to dream big and work towards a brighter future.


YEAR EVENT
1931 Born on October 15 in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India.
1954 Graduated in Physics from St. Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli.
1955 Completed his graduation in Aerospace Engineering from the Madras Institute of Technology (MIT).
1960s Joined the Indian National Committee for Space Research (INCOSPAR) under Vikram Sarabhai at ISRO.
1969 Played a pivotal role in the launch of India’s first satellite launch vehicle, SLV-3.
1974 Developed the first indigenous satellite launch vehicle, PSLV, which was crucial for India's space capabilities.
1980 Played an important role in the launch of India's first satellite, Rohini, into orbit.
1992 Appointed as the Chief Scientific Adviser to the Prime Minister and Secretary of the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO).
1998 Played a major role in India's nuclear tests at Pokhran, known as "Operation Shakti".
2001 Became the 11th President of India, serving from 2002 to 2007.
2002 Elected as President of India.
2007 Completed his term as President of India. Continued to be active in educational and scientific endeavors.
2011 Released his book "Transcendence: My Spiritual Experience with Pramukh Swamiji."
2015 Died on July 27, 2015, while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management, Shillong.
Posthumous Remains a beloved figure in Indian history for his contributions to space research, nuclear science, and nation-building.

Achievements

  • Missile Development:
                     Played a crucial role in the development of India's missile programs, including the Agni and Prithvi missiles.

  • Nuclear Tests:
                    Instrumental in conducting India’s nuclear tests in 1998, making India a nuclear power.

  • ISRO Contributions:
                     Worked with ISRO (Indian Space Research Organisation) and helped launch India's first satellite launch vehicle, SLV-3.

  • Presidency:
                     Served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007, gaining the title "The People's President" for his connection with the public.

  • Youth Inspiration:
                     Motivated millions of young Indians to pursue careers in science, technology, and education through his speeches and books.

  • Writings:
                     Authored best-selling books like "Wings of Fire", "Ignited Minds", and "India 2020" that continue to inspire generations.

  • Vision for India:
                     Advocated for a knowledge-based economy, focusing on education, research, and technological advancements for India's progress.

  • Bharat Ratna:
                     Awarded the Bharat Ratna, India’s highest civilian honor, in 1997 for his contributions to science and technology.

  • Advocate for Education:
                     Championed quality education and the empowerment of the youth to build a better future.

  • Global Recognition:
                     Received numerous international honors and recognitions for his work in science, technology, and public service.

Medals,Awards and Honors

  1. Bharat Ratna (1997):
                     India's highest civilian award, honoring his exceptional contribution to science and public service.

  2. Padma Vibhushan (1990):
                     India's second-highest civilian award, awarded for his work in defense technology and aerospace.

  3. Padma Bhushan (1981):
                     India's third-highest civilian award, recognizing his contributions to science and engineering.

  4. Hoover Medal (2009):
                     Prestigious American engineering honor for his contributions to public service and improving quality of life.

  5. Von Braun Award (2013):
                     Given by the National Space Society (NSS), recognizing his work in advancing space technology.

  6. Indira Gandhi Award for National Integration (1997):
                     Acknowledging his efforts to promote national integration through his visionary leadership.

  7. International von Karman Wings Award (2009):
                     Presented by the California Institute of Technology for his achievements in aerospace engineering.

  8. King Charles II Medal (2007): Awarded by the Royal Society, UK, for his contributions to science and technology in India.
