About

Ratan Naval Tata, born on December 28, 1937, is one of India's most respected industrialists and philanthropists. He is the Chairman Emeritus of Tata Sons and led the Tata Group, India's largest conglomerate, from 1991 to 2012. Under his leadership, the Tata Group expanded globally and diversified into industries such as automobiles, steel, IT, and tea.Key milestones include the acquisition of iconic brands like Jaguar Land Rover, Tetley Tea, and Corus Steel, which transformed Tata Group into a global powerhouse. Ratan Tata is also credited with launching the Tata Nano, the world’s most affordable car, aimed at improving the lives of India's middle-class families.Apart from his business achievements, Ratan Tata is widely admired for his philanthropy. He has dedicated a significant portion of Tata Group's profits to social causes, including education, healthcare, and rural development. Through Tata Trusts, he has supported initiatives that impact millions of lives across India.Known for his humility and integrity, Ratan Tata is a recipient of numerous national and international awards, including the Padma Bhushan and Padma Vibhushan, India’s highest civilian honors. His legacy reflects a commitment to ethical leadership, innovation, and improving society.


YEAR EVENT
1937 Born on December 28 in Bombay (now Mumbai), India.
1962 Joined the Tata Group, starting on the shop floor of Tata Steel in Jamshedpur.
1971 Appointed Director-in-Charge of the National Radio & Electronics Company Limited (NELCO).
1981 Named Chairman of Tata Industries, the Group's other holding company.
1991 Took over as Chairman of Tata Group, succeeding J.R.D. Tata.
1998 Launched the Tata Indica, India's first indigenously designed and manufactured car.
2000 Tata Tea (now Tata Consumer Products) acquired Tetley, marking a major global acquisition.
2001 Tata Group played a significant role in relief efforts for the Gujarat earthquake.
2004 Tata Motors acquired Daewoo Commercial Vehicles in South Korea.
2007 Acquired Corus Group (now Tata Steel Europe), the biggest acquisition by an Indian company.
2008 Acquired Jaguar Land Rover (JLR), marking a historic moment for the Tata Group.
2009 Launched the Tata Nano, the world's most affordable car.
2012 Retired as Chairman of Tata Group; succeeded by Cyrus Mistry.
2016 Temporarily returned as Interim Chairman after Cyrus Mistry's removal.
2017 N. Chandrasekaran took over as Chairman of Tata Sons.
2021 Ratan Tata's investment in startups like Ola Electric and Urban Company gained attention.
2022 Honored with the prestigious Padma Bhushan for his contributions to trade and industry.

Achievements

  • Leadership of Tata Group:
                Ratan Tata served as the chairman of the Tata Group from 1991 to 2012 and again from October 2016 to February 2017. Under his leadership, the company transformed into a global conglomerate with significant expansions.

  • Global Acquisitions:
                Spearheaded key international acquisitions such as:            
                Jaguar Land Rover (2008)
                Corus Steel (2007)
                Tetley Tea (2000)
                These moves positioned Tata Group as a global leader in various industries.
  • Development of the Tata Nano:
                Introduced the Tata Nano in 2008, aimed at providing affordable transportation for middle-class families in India.

  • Philanthropic Initiatives
                Promoted education, healthcare, and rural development through Tata Trusts, which control over 66% of Tata Group profits for charitable purposes.

  • Visionary Leadership
                Played a pivotal role in modernizing the Tata Group and streamlining its diverse businesses.

  • Promoter of Innovation
                Encouraged and supported technology-driven solutions like Tata Consultancy Services (TCS) and Tata Motors' electric vehicles.

Awards:

                India's second-highest civilian award, recognizing his exceptional contributions to trade and industry.

                For his contribution to strengthening economic ties between India and Singapore.

  7. Carnegie Medal of Philanthropy (2007):
                Honoring his dedication to philanthropy and improving quality of life.

  8. Oslo Business for Peace Award (2010):
                Recognized for ethical business practices and inspiring leadership.

  9. CNN-IBN Indian of the Year (2011):
                For his impact on business and society.

                Presented by the Canada India Foundation for his efforts to strengthen India's position on the global stage.
