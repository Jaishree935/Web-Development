About

Mahendra Singh Dhoni, popularly known as MS Dhoni or "Captain Cool," is one of the greatest Indian cricketers and captains in cricket history. Known for his calm demeanor and exceptional leadership skills, Dhoni led India to numerous historic victories. A wicketkeeper-batsman, he revolutionized Indian cricket with his fearless finishing abilities and sharp cricketing mind.


YEAR EVENT
2004 Made his international debut in ODI cricket against Bangladesh on December 23
Scored his maiden ODI century (148 runs) against Pakistan in Visakhapatnam
2007 Appointed captain of the Indian T20 team and led them to victory in the inaugural ICC T20 World Cup.
Emerged as a dependable finisher, securing multiple games with his calm and composed batting.
2010 Guided India to become the No. 1 Test team in ICC rankings for the first time.
Played a pivotal role in key Test series wins against Australia and South Africa.
2011 Captained India to a historic ICC Cricket World Cup victory, hitting the iconic winning six in the final.
Scored 91* in the World Cup final against Sri Lanka, earning the Player of the Match award.
2013 Led India to win the ICC Champions Trophy, defeating England in the final.
Became the only captain to win all three ICC trophies (T20 World Cup, ODI World Cup, and Champions Trophy).
2020 Announced his retirement from international cricket on August 15, marking the end of an era.
Left a legacy as India’s most successful captain with 27 Test, 110 ODI, and 41 T20I wins.

Achievements

  • Captained India to three major ICC trophies:
                T20 World Cup (2007), ODI World Cup (2011), and Champions Trophy (2013)—a feat unmatched by any other captain in cricket history.

  • Record as a Wicketkeeper
                Holds the record for the most stumpings in international cricket (195) and most dismissals by a wicketkeeper in ODIs and T20Is.
  • Indian Premier League (IPL) Success
                Dhoni led Chennai Super Kings (CSK) to 3 IPL titles (2010, 2011, 2018) and 2 Champions League T20 titles (2010, 2014).

  • Most Successful Indian Captain in ODI History
                Under his captaincy, India won 110 ODIs (the most by any Indian captain), including crucial series wins against major teams.

Awards:

  1. Padma Bhushan (2018):
                India’s third-highest civilian award for his unparalleled contribution to Indian cricket.

  2. Padma Shri (2009):
                India’s fourth-highest civilian honor for excellence in sports.

  3. Rajiv Gandhi Khel Ratna (2007–2008):
                The highest sporting honor in India, recognizing his achievements in cricket.

  4. ICC ODI Player of the Year (2008, 2009):
                The first player to win this prestigious award consecutively for his outstanding performances in ODIs.
